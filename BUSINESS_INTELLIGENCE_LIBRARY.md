- **Problem**: Low standards, mediocrity tolerated
- **Solution**: Peer-to-peer accountability
- **Exercises**: Team effectiveness exercise, publication of goals
- **Result**: High performance culture

**Dysfunction 5: Inattention to Results** (Top)
- **Problem**: Focus on individual success over team
- **Solution**: Collective goals and public scoreboard
- **Exercises**: Results-based rewards, team objectives

## ðŸ“– Book 9: $100M Closing Playbook

**Author**: Alex Hormozi  
**Size**: 8.64 MB  
**Type**: Sales Closing System

### What It's About:
Comprehensive framework for closing high-ticket sales through objection handling, framing, and psychological persuasion. Covers both the mechanics and mindset of elite closers.

### The Closing Framework:

**1. Pre-Frame (Before the Call)**
- Set expectations in advance
- Send pre-call materials
- Qualify budget and authority
- Create scarcity (limited slots)

**2. Rapport & Discovery (First 5-10 mins)**
- Build genuine connection
- Ask open-ended questions
- Listen more than talk (80/20 rule)
- Identify true pain points

**3. Value Presentation (Next 10-15 mins)**
- Present solution tied to THEIR problem
- Use stories and case studies
- Demonstrate ROI/value
- Stack value > price

**4. Handling Objections (Ongoing)**

**Common Objections + Responses:**

**"I need to think about it"**
- Reality: They're not convinced
- Response: "What specifically do you need to think about?"
- Dig deeper, address real concern

**"It's too expensive"**
- Reality: They don't see the value
- Response: "Too expensive compared to what?"
- Reframe: Cost vs. value, cost of inaction

**"I need to talk to my spouse/partner"**
- Reality: Not the decision maker OR avoiding
- Response: "What would they want to know?"
- Address concerns, offer to include them

**"I don't have the money"**
- Reality: It's not a priority
- Response: "If money wasn't an issue, would you do it?"
- Then: Payment plans, value justification

**"I need more time"**
- Reality: Fear or uncertainty
- Response: "What would change in a week?"
- Create urgency: bonuses, price increase, limited spots

**5. The Close (Final Push)**
- Assume the sale
- Use trial closes throughout
- Ask for the order directly
- Stay silent after asking (first to speak loses)
- Handle last-minute resistance

### Advanced Closing Techniques:

**The Columbo Close:**
- "Just one more thing..." after they say no
- Brings up the real objection they're hiding

**The Puppy Dog Close:**
- Let them try before they buy
- Reduces risk, increases commitment
- "Take it home for a week"

**The Alternative Close:**
- Don't ask IF, ask WHICH
- "Would you prefer option A or option B?"
- Assumes they're buying, just picking version

**The Summary Close:**
- Recap all value and benefits
- "So let me make sure I understand..."
- Get agreement on each point
- Natural transition to "So let's get started"

### Mindset & Psychology:

**Beliefs of Elite Closers:**
- You're helping them, not selling them
- Objections are questions in disguise
- Every no brings you closer to yes
- You deserve to be paid well
- Confidence = conviction in your solution

**Tonality & Delivery:**
- Match and mirror their energy
- Use pauses for emphasis
- Slow down when discussing price
- Speed up when handling objections
- Vary tone to maintain engagement

### Best For:
- Sales professionals (B2B, high-ticket B2C)
- Consultants and coaches
- Agency owners
- Anyone closing $1K+ deals

### Actionable Tactics:
- Record and review your sales calls
- Role-play objections daily
- Have 3 closes ready (primary, backup, nuclear)
- Always ask for the order
- Follow up minimum 5-7 times
- Use social proof (testimonials, results)
- Create urgency (bonuses expire, prices increase)

**The Money Line:**
> "If I could show you how to [get their desired outcome], would you be interested in getting started today?"
